+++++ AC vs. FC +++++



Article count (AC) - Regardless of whether an article has one or more authors, each author's country or institution receives an AC score.


Fractional measurement (FC) - FC considers the relative contribution of each author of the paper. An article has a total FC score of 1, and the score is shared equally by all authors, assuming that each person's contribution is the same. For example, a paper has ten authors, and each author has an FC score of 0.1. If the author has multiple work units, then their personal FC scores will be evenly distributed among these work units.


Although AC and FC represent the academic power to some extent, there is still diffenrence in the 2 clouds. The larger the font size, the higher scores of AC or FC will be. Although some universities has a lower AC index, such as Peking university, its FC index is going to be higher. It might suggest that Peking University tends to publish an article in a more group work method.
